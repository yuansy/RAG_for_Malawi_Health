| Why is it important to estimate carrier numbers for viral hepatitis B and C? | Estimating carrier numbers for viral hepatitis B and C is important because it helps in understanding the burden of chronic infection, which can lead to severe sequelae such as cirrhosis and hepatocellular carcinoma. This information can be used to plan and monitor prevention and control measures, including vaccination, screening, and treatment. | TG Booklet 6 \| Section: Acute and chronic viral hepatitis \| Paragraph: 125 | Correct samples |
| Where do large outbreaks of Bacterial Meningitis occur, and how do they differ from smaller outbreaks outside the meningitis belt? | Large outbreaks of Bacterial Meningitis occur in the African meningitis belt, an area that extends from Senegal to Ethiopia. These outbreaks are typically associated with Neisseria meningitidis serogroup A before the introduction of a meningococcal A conjugate vaccine (MACV) and may occur during the dry season (November through May). Outside of the meningitis belt, smaller outbreaks may occur year-round. | TG Booklet 6 \| Section: Bacterial Meningitis \| Paragraph: 143 | Correct samples |
| What are the surveillance goals for lymphatic filariasis, and how can they be implemented at the local and international levels? | The surveillance goal for lymphatic filariasis is to confirm community prevalence of infection by surveys. This can be implemented at the local level through routine monthly reporting of aggregated data on probable and confirmed cases from periphery to intermediate level and to central level, sentinel population surveys (standardized and periodical), and active case-finding through surveys of selected groups or through mass surveys. | TG Booklet 6 \| Section: Lymphatic Filariasis \| Paragraph: 203 | Correct samples |
| How should Monkeypox specimens be prepared, stored, and transported? | MonkeyPox specimens should be collected in adequate quantity into appropriate containers at the healthcare facility level or, if necessary, in the field during an outbreak investigation. All specimens must be triple packaged and labelled correctly and accompanied with the correct laboratory forms in order to arrive at the laboratory in good condition, and provide reliable results. | TG Booklet 6 \| Section: MonkeyPox \| Paragraph: 234 | Correct samples |
| Compare the purposes and criteria for conducting a register review with those of the District log of suspected outbreaks and alerts. | The purpose of a register review is to collect information on cases admitted to the health facility during a specific period, whereas the District log of suspected outbreaks and alerts aims to record verbal or written information from health facilities or communities about suspected outbreaks, alerts, or reports of unexplained events. The criteria for conducting a register review include reviewing inpatient registers for paediatric and infectious disease wards, ensuring that the minimum information is collected (name, signs and symptoms, date of onset of symptoms, outcome, and immunization status), and identifying sources of information. In contrast, the District log of suspected outbreaks and alerts has specific criteria such as recording the condition or disease, source of suspected outbreak or rumour, number of cases initially reported, location, and date district was notified. | TG Booklet 3 \| Section: Annex 4D: How to conduct a register review \| Paragraph: 419 <br> TG Booklet 3 \| Section: Annex 4A: District log of suspected outbreaks and alerts \| Paragraph: 411 | Correct samples (multiple chunks) |
